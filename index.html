üöÄ Characteristics

Retrofuturism has two overlapping trends: the future as seen from the past and the past as seen from the future.

The first one is inspired by the imagined future in the 1960s. The visions of future were in the projections of existing technology, or science fiction novels. These visions are updated for the present, and offer a nostalgic image of what the future might have been.

The second one is the futuristic retro. One great example of this is steampunk, which is the application of neo-Victorian styles to modern technology. It starts with the retro content, and then mix it with futuristic technology.

üèô Background

The artworks of retro-futurism also explore the tension between past and future, and between the alienating and empowering effects of technology.

Retrofuturism was shaped in the 1970s when there were rapid and intense changes in technology. The computers were invented, and the future looked promising. The futurists of that time period had some visions like the space colonies, robotic servants, and interstellar travel. However, retrofuturism sees these visions from a skeptical perspective. These visions may never come true, and at the same time, with the Vietnam war and the energy crisis, people started to question the benefits of technology. The anticipated future will not be realized, and technology may not improve our civilization.

Under this circumstance, retrofuturism becomes an ideology, which is the future does not exist except as an act of belief or imagination.
